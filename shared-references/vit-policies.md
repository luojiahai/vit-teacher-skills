# VIT Policies and Requirements

## Overview

The Victorian Institute of Teaching (VIT) is an independent statutory authority that regulates the teaching profession in Victoria. Its primary function is to ensure that all registered teachers meet the standards for professional practice.

Source: Victorian Institute of Teaching — https://www.vit.vic.edu.au/

---

## Registration Categories

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **Provisional** | Graduate teachers in their first years of practice. Must move to full registration within 2 years. |
| **Full** | Teachers who have demonstrated the APST at the Proficient level. |
| **Non-practising** | Teachers not currently teaching but maintaining registration. |
| **Permission to Teach** | Authorised individuals without full teaching qualifications (time-limited). |
| **Casual Relief** | Teachers working on a casual basis. |
| **Dual** | Registered as both an early childhood teacher and a school teacher. |
| **Experienced Returning** | Pathways for teachers returning to the profession after an absence. |

---

## Professional Learning (PL) Requirements

### For fully registered teachers
- **20 hours** of professional learning per year
- Must cover at least **one descriptor from each of the 3 APST domains** annually
- Activities can include: seminars, conferences, workshops, online learning, professional reading, research participation, post-graduate study
- PL must be linked to the teacher's **Professional Practice and Learning Plan**

### For provisionally registered teachers
- Professional learning is embedded in the **Inquiry process** (moving to full registration)
- Focus on developing practice from Graduate to Proficient level

---

## Recency of Practice

- Fully registered teachers must undertake **20 days of professional practice** within the preceding registration period
- Can include: teaching, equivalent practice, or educational leadership
- Equivalent practice may include: curriculum development, education policy, assessment development, professional learning delivery, education research

---

## Registration Renewal

- Annual renewal required for all registration categories
- Must maintain:
  - Current criminal history check
  - Working with Children Clearance
  - Recency of practice (20 days/year)
  - Professional learning (20 hours/year)
- Renewal via the **MyVIT** portal

---

## Audit

- VIT conducts audits to verify teachers' self-declarations
- Audited items: professional learning, recency of practice
- Teachers must provide evidence linking PL activities to specific APST descriptors
- Evidence must be retained for a **minimum of 2 years**

## Codes of Conduct and Ethics

### VIT Code of Conduct
Teachers must:
- Act with integrity, impartiality, and in the best interests of students
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Comply with legal obligations (mandatory reporting, child safety)
- Not engage in misconduct or conduct unbecoming of a teacher

### Child Safety
- **Mandatory reporting** obligations under the Children, Youth and Families Act 2005 (s.184)
- **Failure to Disclose** (Crimes Act 1958, s.327) — all adults must report sexual offences against children under 16
- **Failure to Protect** (Crimes Act 1958, s.49O) — people in authority must remove/reduce risk of sexual abuse
- Ministerial Order No. 870 — Child Safe Standards
- **Four Critical Actions** framework for responding to child abuse incidents

## Provisional to Full Registration — The Inquiry Process

All provisionally registered teachers must complete the VIT Inquiry to demonstrate the APST at the Proficient level.

### Requirements
- Teach at least **80 days** in an Australian or New Zealand school/early childhood setting
- Demonstrate all **37 APST descriptors** at Proficient level
- Complete a **Professional Responsibilities Report**
- Present to a **Workplace Recommendation Panel**

### The 5 Stages (based on Timperley's inquiry model)

1. **Establish content and context** — Understand workplace, learners, program of learning
2. **Develop inquiry question and professional learning** — SMART question, professional conversations (min 2 inquiry + 1 child safety), professional learning
3. **Develop action plan** — Goals, strategies, resources, assessment, inclusive practice
4. **Implement action plan** — Teach the sequence, minimum 3 observations by mentor/experienced colleague
5. **Evaluate effectiveness** — Reflect on learner outcomes and impact on practice

### Evidence types
- Unit/lesson plans (annotated)
- Learner work samples (annotated)
- Assessment data and rubrics
- Observation notes and templates
- Meeting/conversation records
- Parent/carer communication
- Photos, video, or audio of learning
- Professional reading notes

## When Referencing VIT Policies

When using this reference, always:
1. Check the **registration category** of the teacher
2. Reference the relevant **APST standard/descriptor**
3. Note whether the documentation will be **auditable** (retainable evidence)
4. For mandatory reporting: confirm the specific **legal obligation** that applies
5. Stay current — VIT policies are updated periodically; the VIT website is the authoritative source
