# VIT Policies and Requirements

## Overview

The Victorian Institute of Teaching (VIT) is the statutory authority for the regulation of the teaching profession in Victoria, established under the *Education and Training Reform Act 2006*. VIT registers teachers, approves initial teacher education programs, and regulates the professional conduct of teachers.

**Source**: VIT (vit.vic.edu.au), *Education and Training Reform Act 2006* (Vic)

## Registration Categories

VIT issues the following registration categories:

| Category | Who | Key requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Provisional** | New graduates; teachers returning after 5+ years away | Must complete the Inquiry process to move to full registration |
| **Full** | Teachers who have demonstrated the APST at Proficient level | Annual renewal with 20 hours PL and recency of practice |
| **Non-practising** | Teachers not currently teaching but maintaining registration | No PL or recency requirement; cannot teach |
| **Permission to Teach (PTT)** | Unregistered individuals employed to fill a teaching position | Granted to specific school/position; time-limited |

Dual registration (holding both provisional/full and PTT, or early childhood and school) is also possible. Casual relief teachers (CRTs) hold provisional or full registration.

## Provisional to Full Registration — The VIT Inquiry

Provisionally registered teachers (PRTs) must complete the **VIT Inquiry** process to achieve full registration. Key requirements:

### Eligibility
- Hold provisional registration
- Have completed at least **80 days** of teaching practice (not pre-service placements)
- Be employed in a Victorian school
- Have a recommendation from a Workplace Recommendation Panel

### The Inquiry Process (5 Stages)
Based on Professor Helen Timperley's inquiry model:

1. **Stage 1: Establish content and context** — Document your workplace, cohort, and focus learners
2. **Stage 2: Develop inquiry question and professional learning** — Frame a SMART inquiry question; undertake and document professional learning
3. **Stage 3: Develop the action plan** — Plan the teaching sequence, assessment, and observations
4. **Stage 4: Implement the action plan** — Teach, collect evidence, be observed (minimum 3 times)
5. **Stage 5: Evaluate effectiveness** — Analyse impact, map evidence to all 37 APST descriptors, write the Professional Responsibilities Report

### Workplace Recommendation Panel
School-convened panel comprising:
- Chair (Principal or delegate)
- A mentor-trained teacher
- A colleague who knows the PRT's work

The panel reviews the Inquiry evidence and recommends to VIT whether the PRT has demonstrated the APST at the Proficient level.

### Evidence Retention
All Inquiry evidence must be retained for at least **2 years** after achieving full registration. VIT may audit.

## Annual Registration Renewal

All teachers with full registration must renew annually via MyVIT:

### Professional Learning (PL)
- **20 hours** minimum per registration year
- Must address at least one descriptor from **each of the 3 APST domains**
- PL must be linked to professional practice and learning goals
- PL records must be retained for at least **2 years** (audit-ready)

### Recency of Practice
- Must have completed at least **20 days** of teaching practice (or equivalent professional practice as defined by VIT) in the preceding registration period
- For teachers returning after 5+ years: may need to re-enter at provisional registration

### What Counts as Professional Practice
VIT considers the following as professional practice:
- Teaching in a school or early childhood service
- Educational leadership roles (principal, assistant principal, leading teacher)
- Teaching-related roles in universities or education organisations
- Professional learning activities (workshops, conferences — though these primarily count as PL, not practice)

## VIT Audits

VIT conducts random audits of:
- **Registration applications** — verifying qualifications, identity, and suitability
- **Annual renewal declarations** — verifying PL hours, recency of practice, and APST coverage
- **Full registration evidence** — post-Inquiry audits up to 2 years after full registration

If audited, teachers must provide evidence supporting their declarations. Failure to provide adequate evidence may result in conditions on registration or referral for investigation.

## Codes of Conduct and Ethics

### The Victorian Teaching Profession's Code of Conduct
Establishes the standards of professional conduct expected of all registered teachers:
- **Professional integrity**: Honesty, objectivity, and impartiality in all professional dealings
- **Respect**: Treating students, colleagues, parents, and the community with respect and dignity
- **Responsibility**: Acting in the best interests of students; maintaining professional boundaries
- **Competence**: Maintaining and developing professional knowledge and practice

### The Victorian Teaching Profession's Code of Ethics
Articulates the ethical principles that underpin the teaching profession:
- Commitment to students and their learning
- Commitment to the profession
- Commitment to the community

Breaches of the Codes may result in VIT investigation and disciplinary action, including conditions on registration, suspension, or cancellation.

## Teacher Conduct and Suitability

VIT assesses and investigates:
- **Criminal history**: All teachers must undergo a Nationally Coordinated Criminal History Check (NCCHC) and report any changes in criminal history
- **Working with Children**: Teachers must hold a current WWC Clearance (unless exempt)
- **Serious misconduct**: Schools must report conduct matters to VIT
- **Competence concerns**: Issues with professional competence may be referred to VIT

Teachers must self-report:
- Any criminal charges or convictions
- Any cancellation or suspension of WWC clearance
- Any disciplinary findings by another regulatory body
- Any significant health issues that may affect fitness to teach

## Regulatory Framework

The key legislation governing teacher registration in Victoria:
- *Education and Training Reform Act 2006* (Vic) — establishes VIT and its functions
- *Education and Training Reform Regulations 2017* — details registration categories, fees, and processes
- Ministerial Orders — including those relating to child safety and registration requirements
