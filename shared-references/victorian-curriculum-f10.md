# Victorian Curriculum F–10

## Overview

The Victorian Curriculum F–10 sets out what every student should learn during their first eleven years of schooling. It incorporates the Australian Curriculum and reflects Victorian priorities and standards. **Version 2.0** is the current version, with English and Mathematics mandatory from 2025 (Mathematics from Term 1 2026 for F–10).

Source: Victorian Curriculum and Assessment Authority (VCAA) — https://f10.vcaa.vic.edu.au/

---

## Structure

The curriculum is organised as a **continuum across levels of learning achievement** (not years of schooling):

| Band | Levels |
|---|---|
| Towards Foundation | Levels A, B, C, D |
| Foundation | Level F |
| Primary | Levels 1–6 |
| Secondary | Levels 7–10 |
| Advanced | Level 10A |

---

## The 8 Learning Areas

1. **The Arts** — Dance, Drama, Media Arts, Music, Visual Arts, Visual Communication Design
2. **English** — including English as an Additional Language (EAL)
3. **Health and Physical Education**
4. **The Humanities** — Civics and Citizenship, Economics and Business, Geography, History
5. **Languages** — 19 languages including Auslan, Chinese, French, German, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Spanish, Victorian Aboriginal Languages, Vietnamese, and others
6. **Mathematics** — Number, Algebra, Measurement, Space, Statistics, Probability (Version 2.0 strands)
7. **Science** — Science Understanding, Science as a Human Endeavour, Science Inquiry Skills
8. **Technologies** — Design and Technologies, Digital Technologies

---

## The 4 Capabilities

Distinct from the Australian Curriculum, the Victorian Curriculum explicitly provides content descriptions and achievement standards for four capabilities:

1. **Critical and Creative Thinking**
2. **Ethical Capability**
3. **Intercultural Capability**
4. **Personal and Social Capability**

These are taught explicitly **in and through** the learning areas.

---

## Achievement Standards

### Structure by learning area

- **English and Mathematics**: Achievement standards in **11 levels** (F–10)
- **All other learning areas and capabilities**: Achievement standards in **5–6 bands** (e.g., F–2, 3–4, 5–6, 7–8, 9–10)
- In Version 2.0, achievement standards are positioned at the **top** of each curriculum level

### What achievement standards describe

Achievement standards describe what students are able to **understand and do**. They are the basis for:
- Planning teaching and learning programs
- Assessing student progress
- Reporting to parents/carers

---

## Mathematics Version 2.0 — Key Changes (from 2025)

- **Strands renamed**: Number, Algebra, Measurement, Space, Statistics, Probability
- **Single aggregated score** for reporting (no separate strand reporting)
- Version 1.0 and 2.0 **cannot be directly compared** — no progress line in first reporting period
- Achievement standards organised into paragraphs reflecting each strand

## The Three Stages of Schooling

| Stage | Years | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundation** | Prep–Year 2 | English, Maths, Health & PE, The Arts, Personal and Social Capability (core); plus Humanities, Science, Technologies drawn on |
| **Breadth** | Years 3–8 | All learning areas and capabilities, emphasis on English, Maths, Science |
| **Pathways** | Years 9–10 | Broad education with planning for senior secondary pathways; English, Maths, Science at each year level |

## VTLM 2.0 — Victorian Teaching and Learning Model

The VTLM 2.0 provides the pedagogical framework for implementing the curriculum. Key elements:

1. **Learning intentions** — clear articulation of what students will learn
2. **Success criteria** — how students will know they've achieved the learning intention
3. **Explicit teaching** — clear explanation, modelled application
4. **Supported application** — guided practice with feedback
5. **Student demonstration** — independent practice showing understanding
