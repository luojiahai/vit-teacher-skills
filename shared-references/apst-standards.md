# Australian Professional Standards for Teachers (APST)

## Overview

The APST are the national standards for Australian teachers, organised into **7 standards** across **3 domains** and **4 career stages**. VIT uses the **Graduate** and **Proficient** levels for teacher registration. All 37 descriptors must be evidenced for full registration via the VIT Inquiry process.

Source: Australian Institute for Teaching and School Leadership (AITSL)

---

## The 3 Domains and 7 Standards

### Domain: Professional Knowledge

**Standard 1 — Know students and how they learn**
- 1.1 Physical, social and intellectual development and characteristics of students
- 1.2 Understand how students learn
- 1.3 Students with diverse linguistic, cultural, religious and socioeconomic backgrounds
- 1.4 Strategies for teaching Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students
- 1.5 Differentiate teaching to meet the specific learning needs of students across the full range of abilities
- 1.6 Strategies to support full participation of students with disability

**Standard 2 — Know the content and how to teach it**
- 2.1 Content and teaching strategies of the teaching area
- 2.2 Content selection and organisation
- 2.3 Curriculum, assessment and reporting
- 2.4 Understand and respect Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people to promote reconciliation between Indigenous and non-Indigenous Australians
- 2.5 Literacy and numeracy strategies
- 2.6 Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

### Domain: Professional Practice

**Standard 3 — Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning**
- 3.1 Establish challenging learning goals
- 3.2 Plan, structure and sequence learning programs
- 3.3 Use teaching strategies
- 3.4 Select and use resources
- 3.5 Use effective classroom communication
- 3.6 Evaluate and improve teaching programs
- 3.7 Engage parents/carers in the educative process

**Standard 4 — Create and maintain supportive and safe learning environments**
- 4.1 Support student participation
- 4.2 Manage classroom activities
- 4.3 Manage challenging behaviour
- 4.4 Maintain student safety
- 4.5 Use ICT safely, responsibly and ethically

**Standard 5 — Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning**
- 5.1 Assess student learning
- 5.2 Provide feedback to students on their learning
- 5.3 Make consistent and comparable judgements
- 5.4 Interpret student data
- 5.5 Report on student achievement

### Domain: Professional Engagement

**Standard 6 — Engage in professional learning**
- 6.1 Identify and plan professional learning needs
- 6.2 Engage in professional learning and improve practice
- 6.3 Engage with colleagues and improve practice
- 6.4 Apply professional learning and improve student learning

**Standard 7 — Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community**
- 7.1 Meet professional ethics and responsibilities
- 7.2 Comply with legislative, administrative and organisational requirements
- 7.3 Engage with the parents/carers
- 7.4 Engage with professional teaching networks and broader communities

---

## Career Stages

| Stage | Typical context |
|---|---|
| **Graduate** | Initial teacher education graduates; demonstrate knowledge and skills to enter the profession |
| **Proficient** | Practising teachers who have met the requirements for full registration; demonstrate successful practice |
| **Highly Accomplished** | Highly effective and skilled practitioners; recognised by colleagues and community |
| **Lead** | Exemplary practitioners who lead and support colleagues; innovate and influence |

VIT registration uses Graduate (provisional registration) and Proficient (full registration).

---

## Using These Standards

When a skill references APST alignment, it means:
1. **Identify** which standards and descriptors the work addresses
2. **Explain** how the work demonstrates the descriptor
3. **Evidence** with specific artefacts (for VIT Inquiry purposes)

Not every document needs to cover all 37 descriptors. The relevant subset depends on context:
- **Lesson planning**: Primarily Standards 1, 2, 3, with elements of 4 and 5
- **Assessment design**: Primarily Standards 5, with elements of 1 and 3
- **Report writing**: Primarily Standard 5.5, with elements of 1, 2.3, 7.3
- **VIT Inquiry**: All 37 descriptors at Proficient level
