# VIT Registration Renewal

This skill helps fully registered Victorian teachers navigate the annual registration renewal process with VIT. It covers the MyVIT renewal declaration, professional learning requirements, recency of practice, and VIT audit preparation.

## Before You Begin

Establish where the teacher is in the renewal cycle:

1. **Registration expiry date** — When does their current registration period end? (Find via MyVIT)
2. **Current PL hours** — How many hours have they recorded? Which APST domains are covered?
3. **Days of teaching** — Have they taught at least 20 days in the current registration period?
4. **Any conduct matters** — Any criminal charges, WWC changes, or other matters to declare?
5. **Audit-ready?** — Are their PL records organised and evidence retained?

Read `shared-references/vit-policies.md` for the full regulatory framework and `shared-references/apst-standards.md` for the standards.

## The Annual Renewal Requirements

To renew full registration annually via MyVIT, teachers must:

1. **Declare** they have completed at least **20 hours** of professional learning
2. **Confirm** their PL addressed at least one descriptor from **each of the 3 APST domains**
3. **Declare** they have completed at least **20 days** of professional practice (recency)
4. **Disclose** any changes to criminal history, WWC clearance, or other conduct matters
5. **Pay** the annual registration fee
6. **Retain** evidence of PL and practice for at least **2 years** (for potential VIT audit)

### Who This Applies To

| Registration Category | Annual Renewal? | PL Requirement? | Recency Requirement? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Full registration** | Yes | 20 hours across 3 domains | 20 days |
| **Provisional registration** | Yes (different process) | PL as part of Inquiry | 80 days before applying for full |
| **Non-practising** | Yes | No | No (but cannot teach) |
| **Permission to Teach (PTT)** | Time-limited; renewal through employer | Varies | Not applicable |

## The MyVIT Renewal Process

### Step 1: Check Your Renewal Date

Your registration period is typically **1 year** from the date of registration or last renewal. VIT sends renewal reminders, but it is your responsibility to renew on time.

- Log in to **MyVIT** at nexus.vit.vic.edu.au
- Your expiry date is shown in your profile
- You can typically renew **up to 6 weeks before** expiry

### Step 2: Verify Your PL Hours

Complete this checklist BEFORE your renewal declaration:

```markdown

| # | Activity | Date | Hours | APST Domain | Descriptor(s) | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Activity name] | [Date] | [X] | [Domain] | [e.g., 1.2, 3.3] | [e.g., Certificate, notes] |
| 2 | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Domain Coverage
- [ ] Professional Knowledge (Standards 1–2): [X] hours — Descriptors: [list]
- [ ] Professional Practice (Standards 3–5): [X] hours — Descriptors: [list]
- [ ] Professional Engagement (Standards 6–7): [X] hours — Descriptors: [list]

### Totals
- Total hours: [X] / 20
- All 3 domains covered: ✓ / ✗
- Evidence retained for each activity: ✓ / ✗
```

### Step 3: Verify Recency of Practice

You must have completed at least **20 days** of professional practice in the current registration period. VIT defines professional practice as:

**What counts** (at least 20 days combined):
- Classroom teaching in a school
- Teaching in an early childhood service (if dual-registered)
- Educational leadership roles (principal, assistant principal, leading teacher)
- Casual relief teaching (CRT)
- Teaching-related roles in universities or approved education settings

**What doesn't count** (towards the 20 days):
- PL activities (workshops, conferences — these count for PL, not practice)
- Non-teaching school roles (administration, IT support, etc.)
- Studying (unless it involves practice teaching)

```markdown
## Recency of Practice Record — [Registration Year]

| Period | School/Setting | Role | Days |
|---|---|---|---|
| Term 1 | [School name] | [Role] | [X] |
| Term 2 | [School name] | [Role] | [X] |
| ... | ... | ... | ... |
| **Total** | | | **[X] / 20** |
```

### Step 4: Prepare Your Declaration

When you submit your annual renewal via MyVIT, you declare that:

1. The information you provide is true and correct
2. You have completed the required PL
3. You meet the recency of practice requirement
4. You have retained evidence
5. You have disclosed all required conduct matters

**Making a false declaration is a serious matter** — it can result in VIT investigation and potential cancellation of registration.

### Step 5: Submit and Pay

- Complete the renewal declaration in MyVIT
- Pay the annual registration fee
- Download and save your confirmation
- Note: Renewal must be completed before your registration expires

## What Happens If You Don't Meet Requirements?

### Insufficient PL Hours
If you haven't completed 20 hours across 3 domains:
- You cannot truthfully complete the renewal declaration
- You should contact VIT before your registration expires
- VIT may grant a short extension in exceptional circumstances
- Do not submit a false declaration — seek advice

### Insufficient Recency of Practice
If you have less than 20 days:
- You may not meet the renewal requirements
- Contact VIT to discuss your circumstances
- Options may include: extension, move to non-practising registration, or provisional registration

### Late Renewal
- If your registration expires, you **cannot legally teach**
- VIT may allow late renewal within a grace period (check current policy)
- After the grace period, you must apply for re-registration (which may require provisional registration)

## VIT Audits

VIT randomly audits renewal declarations. If audited, you must provide evidence supporting your declaration.

### What Auditors Look For

```markdown
## Audit Readiness Checklist

### Professional Learning Evidence
- [ ] PL log with dates, hours, providers, APST links for each activity
- [ ] Certificates of attendance (where available)
- [ ] Notes or reflections on each PL activity
- [ ] Evidence of practice impact — how PL changed your teaching
- [ ] Clear mapping to at least one descriptor from each of the 3 domains

### Recency of Practice Evidence
- [ ] Employment records or statements of service
- [ ] Timetable or teaching schedule
- [ ] CRT day records (if applicable)
- [ ] Leadership role evidence (if applicable)

### Conduct Declarations
- [ ] Any self-notifications submitted to VIT during the period
- [ ] Current WWC clearance details
- [ ] NCCHC (National Criminal History Check) details
```

### Common Audit Failures

1. **Incomplete domain coverage** — hours in only 1 or 2 domains. Most common: all hours in Professional Practice, none in Professional Engagement
2. **PL claimed that isn't PL** — routine staff meetings, regular planning, parent-teacher interviews
3. **No evidence retained** — certificates thrown away, no notes, no reflections
4. **Insufficient recency of practice** — less than 20 days without contacting VIT
5. **False declaration** — claiming hours you didn't complete

## Important Principles

### Why self-audit before declaring
A false declaration on your renewal is treated seriously by VIT. Running a self-audit BEFORE clicking submit prevents accidental misrepresentations. It takes 15 minutes — it's worth it.

### Why all 3 domains matter
It's easy to accumulate PL in Professional Practice (teaching workshops, curriculum PL). Professional Engagement (Standards 6–7) is often overlooked. Consider: professional reading with reflection, mentoring, professional network participation, contributing to curriculum development — these all count and cover Standards 6–7.

### Why evidence retention matters
You need to produce evidence up to 2 years later if audited. Store PL evidence where you can find it — a dedicated folder, a digital portfolio, or a PL log that includes file locations.

### For PRTs
If you're provisionally registered, your renewal process is different. The VIT Inquiry is your pathway to full registration. Your Inquiry professional learning counts toward your PL hours, but the Inquiry is the priority. Use the `vit-inquiry` skill for the Inquiry process.

### If You're Not Currently Teaching
Consider moving to **non-practising registration** if you won't teach for an extended period:
- No PL or recency requirement
- Registration maintained (you remain on the register)
- Cannot teach
- Can return to teaching by applying for provisional or full registration
