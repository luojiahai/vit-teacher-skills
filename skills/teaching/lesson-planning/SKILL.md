# Lesson Planning (Victorian Curriculum / VCE)

This skill helps VIT-registered teachers create single lesson plans aligned to the Victorian Curriculum F–10 or VCE Study Designs. It produces structured, classroom-ready plans that incorporate explicit instruction, differentiation, and APST alignment.

## Before You Begin

Quickly establish what the teacher needs. Ask only what isn't already clear from their request:

1. **Year level** — Which year or band? (e.g., Year 7, Levels F–2, VCE Unit 3)
2. **Learning area / subject** — Victorian Curriculum learning area or VCE subject
3. **Topic** — What content or skill is the focus?
4. **Duration** — How long is the lesson? (default: 60 minutes)
5. **Student context** — Any specific needs, prior knowledge, or adjustments to consider?

If the teacher provides a topic but no year level, ask. The Victorian Curriculum achievement standards vary significantly across levels, and the lesson must target the right one.

If the teacher mentions VCE (Year 11–12), you must reference the relevant Study Design. Read `shared-references/vce-study-designs.md` for VCE structure.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Reference the Curriculum

For **F–10**: Read `shared-references/victorian-curriculum-f10.md` for the curriculum structure. Identify:
- The relevant **learning area** and **level/band**
- The **achievement standard** for that level — this is what students should be able to understand and do
- Specific **content descriptions** that match the topic

For **VCE (11–12)**: Read `shared-references/vce-study-designs.md`. Identify:
- The **subject** and **unit**
- The relevant **Area of Study** and **Outcome(s)**
- **Key knowledge** and **key skills** from the Study Design

If you cannot determine the exact achievement standard or content description, acknowledge this and work from what the teacher tells you — they know their students best.

### Step 2: Align to APST

Read `shared-references/apst-standards.md` for the full standards. Lesson plans primarily address:
- **Standard 1**: Know students and how they learn (differentiation, learner characteristics)
- **Standard 2**: Know the content and how to teach it (content selection, teaching strategies)
- **Standard 3**: Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning (learning goals, sequencing, resources)
- **Standard 4**: Create and maintain supportive and safe learning environments (participation, management)

Note which descriptors the lesson addresses. Be specific (e.g., "APST 3.1 — challenging learning goals" not just "Standard 3").

### Step 3: Design the Lesson

Use the **Victorian Teaching and Learning Model (VTLM 2.0)** structure, which is the Department of Education's endorsed framework:

#### Lesson Plan Structure

```markdown
# [Lesson Title]

**Year Level:** [Year/Band]
**Learning Area:** [Subject]
**Duration:** [X minutes]
**APST Focus:** [List relevant standard descriptors]

## Learning Intention
A single, clear statement of what students will learn (not what they will do).
Begin with: "We are learning to..." or "To understand..."

## Success Criteria
2–4 observable criteria that show students have met the learning intention.
Begin each with: "I can..."

## Victorian Curriculum Alignment
- Achievement standard reference: [Level/Band, learning area]
- Content description(s): [Specific references]
- [For VCE: Area of Study, Outcome, Key Knowledge/Key Skills]

## Lesson Sequence

### Hook (5 mins)
Grab attention, activate prior knowledge, connect to students' lives.
Why this matters: Students learn best when new information connects to existing knowledge and when they see relevance.

### Explicit Teaching — "I Do" (10–15 mins)
Teacher-directed instruction. Explain the concept clearly, model the skill, think aloud. Include:
- Clear explanation of the new concept/skill
- Modelled example (worked example, demonstration, think-aloud)
- Check for understanding (e.g., cold call, mini whiteboards, thumbs up/down)

### Guided Practice — "We Do" (15–20 mins)
Students practice with teacher support. Include:
- Collaborative activity (pairs, small groups, or whole-class)
- Scaffolding that gradually reduces as students gain confidence
- Targeted questioning to check thinking
- Immediate corrective feedback

### Independent Practice — "You Do" (15–20 mins)
Students apply learning independently. Include:
- A task that matches the success criteria
- Differentiated options (see below)
- Clear instructions and expectations

### Plenary (5–10 mins)
Bring the learning together. Include:
- Quick review of the learning intention and success criteria
- Student self-assessment against success criteria
- Exit ticket or reflection question
- Preview of next lesson

## Differentiation
| Student need | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Working above level | [Extension/challenge] |
| Working at level | [Core task] |
| Working below level | [Scaffolded support] |
| EAL learners | [Language support] |
| Additional needs | [Specific adjustments] |

## Resources
- [List all materials, worksheets, technology, equipment needed]

## Assessment Checkpoints
- Formative: [How will you check understanding during the lesson?]
- Summative link: [How does this connect to the unit assessment?]
```

### Step 4: Apply Differentiation Thoughtfully

Differentiation is not three separate worksheets. It's about removing barriers so all students can access the learning. Think about:
- **Content**: same concept, different entry points
- **Process**: same activity, different scaffolds
- **Product**: same learning goal, different ways to demonstrate
- **Environment**: grouping, seating, sensory needs

### Step 5: Connect to Broader Planning

Note how this lesson connects to:
- The unit plan (suggest using the `unit-planning` skill if the teacher hasn't planned the unit yet)
- The assessment schedule (suggest using the `assessment-design` skill for the unit assessment)
- Prior and future lessons in the sequence

## Important Principles

### Why explicit instruction?
Victoria's VTLM 2.0 and the 2025 Annual Implementation Plan emphasise explicit teaching. The evidence is clear: explicit instruction works, especially for introducing new content. The "I Do / We Do / You Do" model is effective because it provides clarity before independence.

### Why learning intentions and success criteria?
Because if students don't know what they're learning or how they'll know they've learned it, they can't take ownership of their learning. Make these visible, referenced during the lesson, and checked at the end.

### Why differentiate?
Because APST 1.5 requires it, and because Victorian classrooms include students with diverse needs. Differentiation is not an add-on — it's core practice.

### Keep it practical
A lesson plan is a guide, not a script. The best plans are clear enough to follow but flexible enough to adapt. Don't over-script. Trust the teacher's professional judgement.
