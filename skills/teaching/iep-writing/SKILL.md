# IEP / ILP Writing (Victorian Schools)

This skill helps VIT-registered teachers write Individual Education Plans (IEPs), Individual Learning Plans (ILPs), and personalised learning documentation for Victorian students. It covers NCCD evidence requirements, EAL plans, high-ability plans, and Middle Years Literacy and Numeracy Support (MYLNS) documentation.

## Before You Begin

Determine what kind of plan is needed:

1. **Plan type** — IEP (disability/additional needs), ILP (general personalised learning), EAL plan, high-ability plan, or MYLNS plan?
2. **Student context** — Year level, known diagnoses, NCCD category (if applicable)
3. **Purpose** — Is this for NCCD evidence, VIT registration audit, internal school use, or parent communication?
4. **Existing documentation** — What assessments, reports, or specialist input already exists?
5. **Review cycle** — How often will this plan be reviewed? (typically termly for IEPs)

If the teacher isn't clear on NCCD categories or requirements, guide them through this first — NCCD evidence must meet specific standards. Read the reference `references/nccd-requirements.md`.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify the Plan Framework

Different plans serve different purposes. Choose the right framework:

| Plan type | Purpose | Key framework |
|---|---|---|
| **IEP (Individual Education Plan)** | Students with disability or significant additional needs | NCCD, Disability Standards for Education 2005 |
| **ILP (Individual Learning Plan)** | Students working significantly above or below curriculum level | School-based framework, curriculum achievement standards |
| **EAL Learning Plan** | Students learning English as an Additional Language | EAL Companion to the Victorian Curriculum |
| **High-Ability Plan** | Students performing well above age-expected standards | Victorian High-Ability Program, DET High-Ability Toolkit |
| **MYLNS Plan** | Years 8–10 students below National Minimum Standard in literacy/numeracy | MYLNS framework, NAPLAN data |

### Step 2: Gather and Document Student Data

A strong plan is evidence-based, not impressionistic:

```markdown
## Student Profile

**Name:** [Student name]
**Year Level:** [Year]
**Plan Type:** [IEP / ILP / EAL / High-Ability / MYLNS]
**Date Created:** [Date]
**Review Date:** [Date — typically end of term]
**Key Contacts:** [Classroom teacher, parent/carer, specialists involved]

### Presenting Levels
#### Academic
- Literacy: [Specific skills, levels, assessment data]
- Numeracy: [Specific skills, levels, assessment data]
- Other learning areas: [As relevant]

#### Social/Emotional/Behavioural
- Strengths: [What does the student do well socially?]
- Challenges: [What specific observable behaviours or patterns need support?]

#### Student Voice
[What does the student say about their own learning? Include their words where possible.]

#### Parent/Carer Input
[What have parents/carers shared about their child's learning at home?]
```

### Step 3: Determine NCCD Category (if applicable)

For students with disability, the Nationally Consistent Collection of Data (NCCD) requires schools to document adjustments. Read `references/nccd-requirements.md` for detailed guidance.

**NCCD categories:**
- Physical
- Cognitive
- Sensory
- Social/Emotional

**NCCD levels of adjustment:**
- **Quality Differentiated Teaching Practice** (QDTP) — adjustments within regular classroom practice
- **Supplementary** — additional support beyond regular practice (e.g., small group work, modified tasks)
- **Substantial** — frequent, significant adjustments (e.g., regular 1:1 support, highly modified curriculum)
- **Extensive** — comprehensive, constant adjustments (e.g., full-time aide support, specialised programs)

The level is determined by the adjustments provided, not by the student's diagnosis. Document what you *do*, not just what the student *has*.

### Step 4: Write SMART Goals

Goals must be SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

```markdown
## Goals

### Goal 1: [Learning Area / Domain]
**Specific:** [Exactly what skill or behaviour will the student develop?]
**Measurable:** [How will we know when the goal is met? What evidence?]
**Achievable:** [Is this goal realistic within the timeframe given current levels?]
**Relevant:** [How does this connect to the Victorian Curriculum or the student's needs?]
**Time-bound:** [By when? Typically end of term.]

**Strategies:**
- [Strategy 1 — what the teacher will do]
- [Strategy 2 — what the student will do]
- [Strategy 3 — what support staff/specialists will do]

**Resources needed:**
- [e.g., visual schedule, text-to-speech software, adjusted worksheets]

**Progress indicators:**
- [Observable evidence that the goal is being met]

### Goal 2: [Next area]
[Same structure — typically 3–5 goals per plan]
```

Important: Goals should be about the student's learning, not about compliance. "Student will complete homework 80% of the time" is a compliance goal. "Student will independently use a graphic organiser to plan their writing" is a learning goal.

### Step 5: Document Adjustments

Adjustments are the specific things the school does differently to support this student's access to learning:

```markdown
## Adjustments and Supports

### Curriculum Adjustments
- [e.g., Modified reading materials at Level 3 rather than Level 5]
- [e.g., Reduced written output — student demonstrates understanding verbally]
- [e.g., Extension tasks in Mathematics — working on Level 8 content]

### Instructional Adjustments
- [e.g., Instructions broken into 2-step chunks with visual support]
- [e.g., Pre-teaching of key vocabulary before each science lesson]
- [e.g., Same learning intention, additional scaffolds during independent practice]

### Environmental Adjustments
- [e.g., Preferential seating near the front, away from windows]
- [e.g., Noise-cancelling headphones available during independent work]
- [e.g., Calm-down corner access with agreed signal]

### Assessment Adjustments
- [e.g., Extra time (10 mins per 60 mins of assessment)]
- [e.g., Scribe for extended writing tasks]
- [e.g., Oral assessment instead of written where appropriate]

### NCCD Evidence Notes
[For NCCD — note the level of adjustment, frequency, and evidence sources. See references/nccd-requirements.md]
```

### Step 6: Plan for Review

```markdown
## Review and Communication

### Review Schedule
- **Fortnightly:** [What will be monitored briefly each fortnight?]
- **Termly:** Formal review of goals and adjustments (date: [X])
- **Annually:** Full plan rewrite or continuation decision (date: [X])

### Communication Plan
- **With student:** How and when will the student discuss their progress? (Student voice is critical — the student should know their goals and be part of reviewing them.)
- **With parents/carers:** How often will updates be provided? What format?
- **With team:** Who else needs to know about this plan? (Specialists, aides, year-level team, leadership)

### SSG (Student Support Group) Meeting Notes
[For students with significant needs — record SSG meeting dates, attendees, and key decisions]
```

## Important Principles

### Why student voice matters
The student should know their goals, understand why they have a plan, and be part of reviewing it. This is not just good practice — it's a Disability Standards requirement. Ask the student: "What helps you learn?" Their answer is often more useful than any specialist report.

### Why goals should be strengths-based, not deficit-based
Focus on what the student can do and what they're building toward. A goal that begins with "Student will stop..." frames the student as a problem. A goal that begins with "Student will develop..." frames them as a learner. Same behaviour, different stance.

### Why NCCD documentation matters
NCCD is not just paperwork — it's how schools receive funding to support students with disability. Poor documentation means poor funding. The evidence must show:
- What adjustments are provided (not just listed, but described with frequency and context)
- That adjustments have been provided for at least 10 weeks
- That the student has been assessed against the plan
- That the plan is reviewed and updated

### EAL learners — age-related scale exception
For EAL students, the standard 5-point age-related reporting scale is not appropriate. EAL students are assessed against the EAL Companion to the Victorian Curriculum, and reports should use a different 5-point scale. Check the school's EAL policy.
