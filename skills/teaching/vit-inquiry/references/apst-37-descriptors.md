# APST — All 37 Descriptors at Proficient Level

## Overview

This reference lists all 37 APST descriptors at the Proficient career stage — the level that provisionally registered teachers must demonstrate through the VIT Inquiry to achieve full registration. Each descriptor includes the full AITSL text and guidance on what evidence might look like.

Source: Australian Institute for Teaching and School Leadership (AITSL)

## Domain: Professional Knowledge

### Standard 1: Know students and how they learn

**1.1 Physical, social and intellectual development and characteristics of students**
> Use teaching strategies based on knowledge of students' physical, social and intellectual development and characteristics to improve student learning.
- **Evidence might include**: Annotated lesson plans showing strategies matched to developmental stages; work samples showing appropriate challenge; differentiation notes

**1.2 Understand how students learn**
> Structure teaching programs using research and collegial advice about how students learn.
- **Evidence**: Unit/lesson plans referencing learning theory; notes from professional conversations about learning; strategies based on educational research

**1.3 Students with diverse linguistic, cultural, religious and socioeconomic backgrounds**
> Design and implement teaching strategies that are responsive to the learning strengths and needs of students from diverse linguistic, cultural, religious and socioeconomic backgrounds.
- **Evidence**: Differentiated resources for EAL learners; culturally inclusive unit content; strategies responsive to specific cultural contexts

**1.4 Strategies for teaching Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students**
> Design and implement effective teaching strategies that are responsive to the local community and cultural setting, linguistic background and histories of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students.
- **Evidence**: Incorporation of Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives; culturally responsive teaching strategies; consultation with Indigenous community members

**1.5 Differentiate teaching to meet the specific learning needs of students across the full range of abilities**
> Develop teaching activities that incorporate differentiated strategies to meet the specific learning needs of students across the full range of abilities.
- **Evidence**: Differentiated lesson materials; IEPs or ILPs implemented in class; extension and support strategies in one lesson

**1.6 Strategies to support full participation of students with disability**
> Design and implement teaching activities that support the participation and learning of students with disability and address relevant policy and legislative requirements.
- **Evidence**: Adjusted learning materials; implementation of Disability Standards requirements; inclusive classroom practices

### Standard 2: Know the content and how to teach it

**2.1 Content and teaching strategies of the teaching area**
> Apply knowledge of the content and teaching strategies of the teaching area to develop engaging teaching activities.
- **Evidence**: Lesson plans showing content knowledge; use of subject-specific teaching strategies; varied and engaging activities

**2.2 Content selection and organisation**
> Organise content into coherent, well-sequenced learning and teaching programs.
- **Evidence**: Unit plans showing logical content progression; scope and sequence documents; learning sequences that build on prior knowledge

**2.3 Curriculum, assessment and reporting**
> Design and implement learning and teaching programs using knowledge of curriculum, assessment and reporting requirements.
- **Evidence**: Unit plans referencing Victorian Curriculum/VCE; assessment tasks aligned to achievement standards; report comments linked to curriculum

**2.4 Understand and respect Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people to promote reconciliation**
> Provide opportunities for students to develop understanding of and respect for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander histories, cultures and languages.
- **Evidence**: Units incorporating Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives; resources used; student activities promoting reconciliation

**2.5 Literacy and numeracy strategies**
> Apply knowledge and understanding of effective teaching strategies to support students' literacy and numeracy achievement.
- **Evidence**: Lesson plans showing explicit literacy/numeracy strategies; differentiated numeracy support; reading comprehension strategies embedded in subject teaching

**2.6 Information and Communication Technology (ICT)**
> Use effective teaching strategies to integrate ICT into learning and teaching programs to make selected content relevant and meaningful.
- **Evidence**: Lessons incorporating ICT; use of digital tools for learning; safe and responsible ICT use

## Domain: Professional Practice

### Standard 3: Plan for and implement effective teaching and learning

**3.1 Establish challenging learning goals**
> Set explicit, challenging and achievable learning goals for all students.
- **Evidence**: Learning intentions and success criteria in lesson plans; goals in IEPs/ILPs; student-articulated goals

**3.2 Plan, structure and sequence learning programs**
> Plan and implement well-structured learning and teaching programs or lesson sequences that engage students and promote learning.
- **Evidence**: Unit plans; coherent lesson sequences; programs that build understanding progressively

**3.3 Use teaching strategies**
> Select and use relevant teaching strategies to develop knowledge, skills, problem-solving and critical and creative thinking.
- **Evidence**: Range of teaching strategies used; explicit instruction practices; activities promoting higher-order thinking

**3.4 Select and use resources**
> Select and/or create and use a range of resources, including ICT, to engage students in their learning.
- **Evidence**: Varied resources used; teacher-created materials; appropriate and engaging resource selection

**3.5 Use effective classroom communication**
> Use effective verbal and non-verbal communication strategies to support student understanding, participation, engagement and achievement.
- **Evidence**: Clear instructions in lesson plans; questioning strategies; communication strategies for diverse learners

**3.6 Evaluate and improve teaching programs**
> Evaluate personal teaching and learning programs using evidence, including feedback from students and student assessment data, to inform planning.
- **Evidence**: Annotated lesson plans showing post-lesson reflection; data analysis leading to program changes; student feedback used to adjust teaching

**3.7 Engage parents/carers in the educative process**
> Plan for appropriate and contextually relevant opportunities for parents/carers to be involved in their children's learning.
- **Evidence**: Parent communication; strategies suggested for home learning; parent-teacher interview preparation linking to learning

### Standard 4: Create and maintain supportive and safe learning environments

**4.1 Support student participation**
> Establish and implement inclusive and positive interactions to engage and support all students in classroom activities.
- **Evidence**: Inclusive classroom strategies; participation data for diverse learners; positive classroom culture indicators

**4.2 Manage classroom activities**
> Establish and maintain orderly and workable routines to create an environment where student time is spent on learning tasks.
- **Evidence**: Classroom routines and procedures; time-on-task data; smooth transitions

**4.3 Manage challenging behaviour**
> Manage challenging behaviour by establishing and negotiating clear expectations with students and addressing discipline issues promptly, fairly and respectfully.
- **Evidence**: Positive Classroom Management Strategies; behaviour records showing consistent responses; de-escalation practices

**4.4 Maintain student safety**
> Ensure students' wellbeing and safety within school by implementing school and/or system, curriculum and legislative requirements.
- **Evidence**: Implementation of child safety requirements; risk assessments; safe learning environment practices

**4.5 Use ICT safely, responsibly and ethically**
> Incorporate strategies to promote the safe, responsible and ethical use of ICT in learning and teaching.
- **Evidence**: Acceptable use agreements; cyber safety lessons; modelling safe ICT use

### Standard 5: Assess, provide feedback and report on student learning

**5.1 Assess student learning**
> Develop, select and use informal and formal, diagnostic, formative and summative assessment strategies to assess student learning.
- **Evidence**: Variety of assessment types; pre- and post-assessment data; assessment tasks aligned to curriculum

**5.2 Provide feedback to students on their learning**
> Provide timely, effective and appropriate feedback to students about their achievement relative to their learning goals.
- **Evidence**: Annotated student work samples with feedback; feedback that identifies next steps; student response to feedback

**5.3 Make consistent and comparable judgements**
> Understand and participate in assessment moderation activities to support consistent and comparable judgements of student learning.
- **Evidence**: Moderation notes; annotated work samples used for moderation; cross-marking records

**5.4 Interpret student data**
> Use student assessment data to analyse and evaluate student understanding of subject/content, identifying interventions and modifying teaching practice.
- **Evidence**: Data analysis leading to teaching changes; identification of students needing intervention; pre/post data showing impact

**5.5 Report on student achievement**
> Report clearly, accurately and respectfully to students and parents/carers about student achievement, making use of accurate and reliable records.
- **Evidence**: Report comments; parent-teacher interview records; student achievement records linking to curriculum standards

## Domain: Professional Engagement

### Standard 6: Engage in professional learning

**6.1 Identify and plan professional learning needs**
> Use the Australian Professional Standards for Teachers and advice from colleagues to identify and plan professional learning needs.
- **Evidence**: Professional learning goals; self-assessment against APST; PL plan linked to identified needs

**6.2 Engage in professional learning and improve practice**
> Participate in learning to update knowledge and practice, targeted to professional needs and school and/or system priorities.
- **Evidence**: PL log; reflections on PL; application of PL in practice

**6.3 Engage with colleagues and improve practice**
> Contribute to collegial discussions and apply constructive feedback from colleagues to improve professional knowledge and practice.
- **Evidence**: Professional conversation notes; feedback from mentor/colleagues applied to practice; PLC/PLT contributions

**6.4 Apply professional learning and improve student learning**
> Undertake professional learning programs designed to address identified student learning needs.
- **Evidence**: PL undertaken in response to student data; evidence of impact of PL on student outcomes; Inquiry process documentation

### Standard 7: Engage professionally with colleagues, parents/carers and the community

**7.1 Meet professional ethics and responsibilities**
> Meet codes of ethics and conduct established by regulatory authorities, systems and schools.
- **Evidence**: Understanding of VIT Code of Conduct; ethical decision-making examples; professional boundaries maintained

**7.2 Comply with legislative, administrative and organisational requirements**
> Understand the implications of and comply with relevant legislative, administrative, organisational and professional requirements, policies and processes.
- **Evidence**: Mandatory reporting knowledge; child safety compliance; administrative record-keeping; policy compliance

**7.3 Engage with the parents/carers**
> Establish and maintain respectful collaborative relationships with parents/ carers regarding their children's learning and wellbeing.
- **Evidence**: Parent communication samples; collaborative parent relationships; parent input incorporated

**7.4 Engage with professional teaching networks and broader communities**
> Participate in professional and community networks and forums to broaden knowledge and improve practice.
- **Evidence**: Professional network involvement; community engagement; broader professional contributions

## Evidence Mapping Strategy

One piece of evidence can (and should) be annotated against multiple descriptors. For example, a single annotated lesson plan might evidence:
- 1.5 (differentiation shown in the plan)
- 2.1 (content knowledge evident)
- 2.3 (curriculum alignment shown)
- 3.1 (challenging learning goals)
- 3.2 (structured sequence)
- 3.3 (teaching strategies used)
- 3.4 (resources selected)
- 4.1 (inclusive participation strategies)

**Annotation is critical** — the evidence itself doesn't speak. Your annotations make the connection between the evidence and the descriptor explicit: "This differentiation shows APST 1.5 because..."

## Key Reminders

- You need to cover all 37 descriptors, but not equally — some will have rich evidence, others may be briefly addressed
- Evidence must be from your practice **as a registered teacher**, not from pre-service placements or non-teaching roles
- The Inquiry is the spine, not the whole body — you can (and should) reference broader practice beyond the Inquiry
- The Workplace Recommendation Panel is not looking for perfection — they're looking for proficient practice and evidence of growth
