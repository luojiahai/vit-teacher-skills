# VIT Inquiry — The 5 Stages in Detail

## Overview

The VIT Inquiry process follows Professor Helen Timperley's model of teacher inquiry and knowledge building. This reference provides detailed guidance for each of the 5 stages, including what to produce, common pitfalls, and tips from successfully registered teachers.

Source: VIT PRT Guide (December 2025 edition)

## Stage 1: Establish Content and Context for Learning

### Purpose
To understand your workplace, your cohort of learners, and the learning program that forms the focus of your Inquiry. This stage sets the scene — it tells the panel *who* you teach, *where* you teach, and *what* your Inquiry is about.

### Key Decisions

**Choosing your focus learners (3–4)**:
Your focus learners are the most important choice in the Inquiry. They should:
- Represent the diversity of your cohort
- Include at least one learner working below the expected level
- Include at least one learner from a priority cohort (EAL, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander, disability/additional needs, Out-of-Home Care)
- Be learners where you can make a visible difference through your teaching

**What makes a good focus learner**:
- You teach them regularly (at least twice per week)
- You know something about their learning needs
- You have (or can collect) baseline data for them
- Their learning challenge is one you can address through your Inquiry

**Choosing your program of learning**:
- Pick a program where you can clearly see learner progress over 4–6 weeks
- Choose content you're confident teaching — the Inquiry is about demonstrating proficiency, not about taking risks with unfamiliar content
- Ensure the program naturally incorporates literacy, numeracy, ICT, and Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander perspectives (these must be addressed somewhere in your Inquiry)

**Common pitfalls**:
- Choosing focus learners who are all similar (doesn't show range)
- Choosing a program that's too broad ("improving writing" is a whole career, not a 6-week Inquiry)
- Skipping the context — the panel needs to understand your setting

### What to Produce
- Description of your education setting
- Description of your cohort
- Profiles of 3–4 focus learners
- Outline of your program of learning with curriculum links

## Stage 2: Develop Inquiry Question and Professional Learning

### Purpose
To frame your Inquiry as genuine professional learning and establish the knowledge base for your action plan.

### Developing Your Inquiry Question

Use VIT's SMART tool. A good question:
- Is specific enough to be answerable in 4–6 weeks
- Identifies a clear teaching strategy or approach
- Names what will be measured and how
- Connects your learning to learner outcomes

**Question formula**: "If I [teaching strategy], will [focus learners] improve in [specific learning outcome] as measured by [evidence]?"

### Professional Learning

Your professional learning should directly inform your action plan. If you claim to have learned something from professional reading but your action plan doesn't reflect it, the panel will notice.

**Minimum requirements**:
- Professional reading (with notes on how it informed your approach)
- At least 1 observation of an experienced colleague (document what you observed, what you learned, what you'll apply)
- At least 3 professional conversations (2 about the Inquiry, 1 about child safety/professional boundaries)

**Documenting professional conversations**:
Don't just log them. Show what you learned:
- "We discussed..." (the topic)
- "I learned..." (the insight)
- "This changed my practice by..." (the impact)
- "As a result, I..." (the action)

**Common pitfalls**:
- Listing PL without showing how it changed your practice
- Professional conversations that are logged but not reflected upon
- An inquiry question that's either too vague to answer or too narrow to generate meaningful evidence

### What to Produce
- Your SMART inquiry question
- Documentation of your professional learning
- Notes from professional conversations
- Notes from observation(s) of experienced colleague(s)

## Stage 3: Develop the Action Plan

### Purpose
To plan the teaching sequence that demonstrates your practice at the Proficient level.

### The Action Plan

Your action plan should show:
- Clear learning goals (for the cohort and for each focus learner)
- Specific teaching strategies informed by your PL
- An assessment plan (pre, formative, summative)
- Inclusive practice — how the plan addresses diverse needs
- Resources needed
- Observation schedule (minimum 3 observations)

**What makes a strong action plan**:
- The strategies are specific (not "use explicit teaching" but *how* you'll use it)
- The assessment plan tells you whether learners improved
- The inclusive practice section shows genuine differentiation, not token adjustments
- The plan is teachable — it's realistic for your context

### Observations Schedule

You need at least 3 observations of your practice by your mentor or experienced colleague. Plan these strategically:
- Observation 1: Early in the sequence (feedback on your approach)
- Observation 2: Mid-sequence (feedback on implementation)
- Observation 3: Late in the sequence (feedback on impact)

**Getting the most from observations**:
- Brief your observer before each observation: "I'd like you to focus on [specific aspect] because..."
- Debrief after: "What did you notice about...?"
- Document the feedback and how you acted on it

### What to Produce
- Action plan document
- Observation schedule
- Evidence of pre-assessment (baseline data)

## Stage 4: Implement the Action Plan

### Purpose
To teach the sequence, collect evidence, and document the process.

### What to Do

**Teach the sequence**: Follow your action plan. Adjust as needed based on formative evidence — flexibility shows responsive teaching (APST 3.6).

**Collect evidence as you go**:
- Lesson plans (annotate with what worked and what you adjusted)
- Learner work samples (annotate with what they show about the learner's progress)
- Observation notes from your mentor
- Your own reflections during the sequence
- Photos or video (with appropriate consent)

**Annotate evidence in real time**: A 30-second annotation written during the lesson ("This work sample shows K's improved ability to structure a paragraph — see the topic sentence followed by supporting details") is worth more than a detailed annotation written from memory a month later.

**What to do during observations**:
- Be observed at least 3 times
- Discuss what the observer noticed
- Document the feedback
- Show how you acted on it

### Common Pitfalls

- Collecting too little evidence and realising too late
- Not annotating evidence — raw work samples don't speak for themselves
- Forgetting to document adjustments made during the sequence
- Not collecting evidence from all focus learners

### What to Produce
- Annotated lesson plans
- Annotated learner work samples
- Observation notes
- Evidence of adjustments and responsive teaching

## Stage 5: Evaluate Effectiveness

### Purpose
To analyse the evidence, reflect on learner outcomes and your practice, and demonstrate proficiency against all 37 APST descriptors.

### The Evaluation

Evaluate at three levels:

1. **Impact on focus learners** — Did they make progress? What's the evidence?
2. **Impact on the broader cohort** — What patterns do you see across the class?
3. **Impact on your practice** — What have you learned? How has your practice moved from Graduate to Proficient?

### APST Evidence Map

Map your evidence across all 37 descriptors. For each descriptor, identify:
- What evidence from the Inquiry addresses it
- What other evidence (from your broader practice) addresses it
- How the evidence demonstrates the descriptor

**The evidence map approach**:
1. List all 37 descriptors
2. Go through your Inquiry evidence and note which descriptors each piece addresses
3. For any gaps, look to your broader practice
4. For any remaining gaps, consider whether you need to add evidence

### The Professional Responsibilities Report

This is a separate, required component covering:
- Understanding of legal obligations (mandatory reporting, duty of care, child safety)
- Understanding of professional boundaries and ethics (VIT Code of Conduct)
- How you maintain a child-safe environment
- Understanding of the Standards and your obligations

Keep this concise but comprehensive. The panel uses this to confirm you understand your professional responsibilities — it's not just a box-ticking exercise.

### Preparing for the Panel

- Organise your evidence so it's accessible (by APST descriptor, not chronologically)
- Be ready to talk about your focus learners — their starting points, their growth, and the teaching that made the difference
- Be prepared to discuss what you learned, not just what you did
- Have examples ready: "Here's a piece of evidence that shows..."
- The panel is a professional conversation, not an exam — they want you to succeed

### What to Produce
- Evaluation of impact on focus learners and cohort
- Reflection on impact on your practice
- APST evidence map (all 37 descriptors)
- Professional Responsibilities Report
- Panel-ready summary

## Timeline

A typical Inquiry takes:
- **Planning (Stages 1–3)**: 1–2 weeks
- **Teaching (Stage 4)**: 4–6 weeks (assuming seeing learners at least twice per week)
- **Evaluation and write-up (Stage 5)**: 1–2 weeks

CRTs and specialist teachers (PE, Languages, Arts) may need longer because they see learners less frequently. The key requirement is teaching the learners for long enough to demonstrate an impact.

## Final Tips

1. **Start collecting evidence from Day 1** — don't leave it to the final week
2. **Annotate as you go** — it takes seconds and saves hours later
3. **Keep your focus learners at the centre** — everything should connect back to their learning
4. **Show your learning, not just your planning** — the Inquiry is about growth
5. **Don't try to be perfect** — the Proficient standard is solid, effective teaching, not flawless teaching
6. **Use your mentor** — they want you to succeed. Ask for help, feedback, and guidance.
7. **The panel is on your side** — they're fellow teachers who want to welcome you to full registration
