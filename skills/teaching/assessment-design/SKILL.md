# Assessment Design (Victorian Curriculum / VCE)

This skill helps VIT-registered teachers design assessment tasks, rubrics, and feedback frameworks aligned to Victorian Curriculum F–10 achievement standards or VCE Study Design outcomes. It covers formative, summative, and diagnostic assessment, with a focus on making consistent and comparable judgements (APST 5.3).

## Before You Begin

Clarify the assessment context:

1. **Purpose** — Formative (checking progress), summative (measuring achievement), or diagnostic (establishing baseline)?
2. **Year level and learning area** — F–10 or VCE?
3. **Topic/unit** — What content or skills are being assessed?
4. **Conditions** — In-class? Take-home? Timed? Open book? Group or individual?
5. **School type** — Government, Catholic, or independent? (affects reporting and moderation)

## Workflow

### Step 1: Map to the Curriculum

For **F–10**: Read `shared-references/victorian-curriculum-f10.md`. Identify:
- The **achievement standard** for the level/band — this defines what success looks like
- Relevant **content descriptions** — these define what's been taught

For **VCE**: Read `shared-references/vce-study-designs.md`. Identify:
- The relevant **Outcome(s)** — these are the assessable statements
- **Key knowledge** and **key skills** — these define the scope of assessment

For **APST alignment**: Read `shared-references/apst-standards.md` — this work primarily addresses Standard 5 (all descriptors).

### Step 2: Determine the Assessment Type

Choose the type that best fits the purpose:

| Type | Best for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Selected response** | Checking recall, basic comprehension | Multiple choice, matching, true/false |
| **Short constructed response** | Checking understanding, application | Short answer, diagram, calculation |
| **Extended response** | Checking analysis, synthesis, evaluation | Essay, research report, argument |
| **Performance task** | Checking application, skills | Lab practical, oral presentation, performance |
| **Product/portfolio** | Checking sustained work, process | Folio, design project, creative work |
| **Conferencing** | Checking thinking, reflection | Reading conference, viva, student-led conference |

For summative assessment: the task must allow students to demonstrate the achievement standard. If the achievement standard says students "analyse and evaluate" and your task only asks them to "describe," the alignment is weak.

### Step 3: Design the Assessment Task

Write the task with these principles:

1. **Clear instructions** — students should understand what to do without teacher clarification
2. **Transparent expectations** — tell students what success looks like (the rubric/criteria should be shared)
3. **Appropriate demand** — the task should match the cognitive level of the achievement standard
4. **Fair access** — consider EAL learners, students with additional needs, reading level of task materials
5. **Manageable scope** — the task should be completable within the stated conditions

```markdown
# [Assessment Title]

**Subject/Learning Area:** [Subject]
**Year Level:** [Year/Band]
**Assessment Type:** [Formative / Summative / Diagnostic]
**Conditions:** [Time allowed, resources permitted, individual/group]
**Victorian Curriculum / VCE Alignment:** [Achievement standard, content descriptions, or Outcome reference]
**APST Alignment:** Primarily Standard 5 (5.1, 5.2, 5.3)

## Task Description
[Clear, student-facing description of what to do]

## Instructions for Students
1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]
3. [...]

## Conditions
- Time: [X minutes]
- Resources permitted: [e.g., calculator, dictionary, notes]
- Submission format: [e.g., handwritten, digital, oral]
```

### Step 4: Build the Rubric

Victorian schools use criterion-based assessment. The rubric should:

- Be based on the **achievement standard** (F–10) or **Outcome** (VCE)
- Use **observable** language — describe what you can see in the work, not what you assume about the student
- Have **distinct levels** — each level should be qualitatively different from the next
- Align with the **Victorian 5-point scale** for reporting where appropriate

```markdown
## Rubric

| Criterion | Well above standard | Above standard | At standard | Below standard | Well below standard |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Criterion 1 from achievement standard] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] |
| [Criterion 2] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] |
| [Criterion 3] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] | [Descriptor] |

### Notes on the scale:
- **At standard** = the student has demonstrated the achievement standard for this level/band
- **Above standard** = the student demonstrates some aspects of the achievement standard for the next level/band
- **Well above** = the student consistently demonstrates the next level's achievement standard
- **Below standard** = the student is working towards the achievement standard
- **Well below** = the student requires significant support to access the achievement standard
```

For formative assessment, a simpler rubric (3 levels) may suffice. For VCE SACs, follow the marking scheme specified in the Study Design.

### Step 5: Design the Feedback Framework

Feedback should answer three questions (aligned to APST 5.2):
1. **Where am I going?** (reference the learning intention/criteria)
2. **How am I going?** (specific observations about their work)
3. **Where to next?** (actionable next steps)

```markdown
## Feedback Framework

### During the task (formative)
- [What will you look for during the task?]
- [What common misconceptions should you watch for?]
- [What prompts will you use to redirect without giving the answer?]

### After the task (summative feedback)
For each student, provide:
1. **What worked**: [Specific strength, referencing the criteria]
2. **What to improve**: [Specific area for growth, referencing the criteria]
3. **Next step**: [One actionable thing the student can do to improve]

### Moderation notes
For consistent judgement (APST 5.3):
- [What evidence characterises each level?]
- [Link to annotated work samples if available]
- [Cross-marking notes for colleagues]
```

## Important Principles

### Why criterion-based, not norm-based?
Victorian assessment is criterion-based: we measure against the achievement standard, not against other students. This is how the Victorian Curriculum and VCE work. The rubric describes what a student at each level can do, independent of where others are.

### Why share the rubric with students?
Because APST 5.2 says feedback should be timely and specific, and because students can't hit a target they can't see. Sharing the rubric before the task means students understand what success looks like. This is especially important for VCE SACs.

### Why moderation matters
APST 5.3 requires consistent and comparable judgements. Two teachers marking the same work should arrive at similar conclusions. Well-designed rubrics with observable language make moderation easier.

### For VCE assessment
VCE SACs and SATs have specific requirements set by VCAA. Always check the current Study Design for:
- Mandatory task types
- Mark allocation
- Authentication requirements
- Conditions (e.g., must be completed in class)
- The Study Design takes precedence over generic advice.
