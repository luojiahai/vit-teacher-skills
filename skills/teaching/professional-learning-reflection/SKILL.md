# Professional Learning Reflection (VIT Registration Renewal)

This skill helps VIT-registered teachers document and reflect on professional learning for annual registration renewal. All fully registered teachers must complete at least 20 hours of professional learning per year, covering all 3 APST domains, and be prepared for potential VIT audit.

## Before You Begin

Understand what's being documented:

1. **Is this for a single PL activity or the full year's log?**
2. **What PL activity/activities?** — Workshops, conferences, online learning, professional reading, research, post-graduate study, collegiate learning
3. **What evidence exists?** — Certificates, notes, attendance records, reading logs
4. **What's the current domain coverage?** — Which APST domains still need hours?

Read `shared-references/vit-policies.md` for the full VIT requirements and `shared-references/apst-standards.md` for the standards.

## The VIT PL Requirements

### The core requirements

- **20 hours** of professional learning per registration year
- Must cover at least **one descriptor from each of the 3 APST domains** (Professional Knowledge, Professional Practice, Professional Engagement)
- PL must be linked to your **professional practice and learning goals**
- Evidence must be retained for at least **2 years** (VIT audit requirement)
- PL can include: seminars, conferences, workshops, online learning, professional reading, research participation, post-graduate study, professional conversations, mentoring, observing colleagues

### What counts as professional learning?

PL must:
- **Enhance** your professional knowledge, practice, or engagement
- Be **linked to the APST**
- Be **additional to** your normal teaching duties (routine planning, marking, and meetings generally don't count — unless they involve documented professional learning)

| Counts as PL | Generally doesn't count |
|---|---|
| Attending a conference on literacy instruction | Regular staff meetings |
| Completing an online course on trauma-informed practice | Routine lesson planning |
| Professional reading with documented reflection | Casual reading without reflection |
| Observing a colleague and discussing practice | Incidental corridor conversations |
| Action research project with documented outcomes | Administering NAPLAN |
| Post-graduate study in education | Parent-teacher interviews |

## Workflow

### Step 1: Document an Individual PL Activity

For each PL activity, document:

```markdown
## PL Activity Record

**Activity title:** [Name of workshop, course, reading, etc.]
**Date(s):** [DD/MM/YYYY or range]
**Duration:** [X hours]
**Provider:** [Organisation, presenter, or source]
**Type:** [Seminar / Conference / Workshop / Online learning / Professional reading / Research / Post-graduate study / Collegiate learning / Observation / Mentoring / Other]

### Activity Description
[2–3 sentences describing what the activity involved]

### APST Alignment
**Primary domain:** [Professional Knowledge / Professional Practice / Professional Engagement]
**Primary standard descriptor(s):** [e.g., 1.2 — Understand how students learn; 3.3 — Use teaching strategies]

### Key Learnings
[3–5 specific things you learned. Be concrete — not "I learned about behaviour management" but "I learned that giving students a positive behaviour script ('When I feel frustrated, I will...') is more effective than telling them what not to do."]

### Impact on Practice
[How will this change what you do in the classroom? Be specific about what you will do differently, with whom, and when.]

### Evidence
[What evidence do you have? Attach or reference: certificate of attendance, notes, annotated slides, reading log entry, observation notes, reflection journal entry.]

### Follow-Up
[Any next steps? E.g., discuss with team, trial a strategy, read further, share with colleagues?]
```

### Step 2: Write a Practice Impact Reflection

The link between PL and practice is what VIT looks for in an audit. Every PL activity should have a "so what?" — how did it change your teaching?

```markdown
## Practice Impact Reflection

**What I learned:**
[Brief summary of the key takeaway from the PL]

**What I tried:**
[What specific strategy, approach, or change did you implement? When? With which class/group?]

**What happened:**
[What did you observe? Student response? Colleague feedback? What worked, what didn't?]

**What I'll do next:**
[Will you continue, modify, or replace this approach? What further learning do you need?]

**Link to learner outcomes:**
[How did this ultimately benefit students? Even if indirect — "I'm now more explicit in my instructions, and I've noticed fewer students asking 'what do I do?' before starting their work."]
```

The practice impact reflection is the difference between "I attended a PD day" (not very useful) and "I attended a PD day, tried the strategy, and here's what happened with my students" (strong PL evidence).

Track all PL across the year, mapped to APST domains:

```markdown
# Professional Learning Log — [Year]

**Teacher:** [Name]

## Summary

| Professional Engagement (Standards 6–7) | Min 1 descriptor | [X hours] | [List descriptors] |

## Activity Log

| # | Date | Activity | Hours | Domain | Key descriptor | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Domain Coverage Check
- [ ] Professional Knowledge — at least 1 descriptor evidenced

```

### Step 4: Audit Preparation

VIT audits are random — any teacher can be selected. If audited, you need to provide evidence for your PL claims. Prepare as if you will be audited:

1. **Keep everything**: Certificates, notes, annotated readings, reflection journals, observation notes
2. **Link clearly**: Every piece of evidence should be clearly connected to a specific APST descriptor
3. **Include practice impact**: The strongest evidence shows not just that you attended something, but that it changed your practice
4. **Check domain coverage**: The most common audit failure is incomplete domain coverage — make sure you have all 3 domains covered
5. **Store accessibly**: You need to retrieve this up to 2 years later. Don't bury PL records in a forgotten folder.

### Step 5: Plan Forward

Use the PL log not just for documentation but for planning:

```markdown
## PL Planning — [Upcoming Year]

### Professional Learning Goals
Based on my practice this year, my PL goals for next year are:
1. [Goal 1 — linked to a specific APST standard/descriptor]
2. [Goal 2]
3. [Goal 3]

### Domain Coverage Plan
- **Professional Knowledge**: [Planned PL activities to cover this domain]
- **Professional Practice**: [Planned PL activities]
- **Professional Engagement**: [Planned PL activities]

### Identified PL Activities
- [Specific workshop, course, reading, or project planned]
- [...]

### Gap Analysis
- [Any descriptors you rarely address? How could you target those?]
```

## Important Principles

### Why document the impact, not just the attendance
VIT's PL requirement is about improving practice, not collecting certificates. The reflection on how PL changed your teaching is the evidence that the PL was meaningful. A folder full of attendance certificates with no practice impact reflections is weak evidence.

### Why domain coverage matters
It's easy to accumulate all your PL hours in one domain (especially Professional Practice). The requirement to cover all 3 domains forces breadth. If you're short on Professional Engagement (Standards 6–7), consider: professional reading with documented reflection, mentoring a colleague, participating in a professional network, or contributing to a curriculum development project.

### Why professional reading counts
Professional reading is one of the most accessible forms of PL — free, flexible, and easy to document. But it only counts if you document what you read and reflect on its impact. A reading log with dates, sources, key takeaways, and practice implications is valid PL evidence.

### Why less can be more
20 hours of deep, practice-changing PL is better than 40 hours of superficial attendance. VIT auditors look for evidence of genuine professional growth, not hour-counting. A single action research project that runs across a term and demonstrably improves student outcomes is stronger evidence than 10 half-day workshops you can't remember.

### For PRTs completing the Inquiry
If you're a provisionally registered teacher completing the VIT Inquiry: your Inquiry professional learning counts toward your PL hours. The Inquiry itself is substantial professional learning. Document it using this skill, but note that the Inquiry documentation (via the `vit-inquiry` skill) is your primary evidence.
