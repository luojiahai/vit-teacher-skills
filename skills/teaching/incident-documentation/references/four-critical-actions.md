# Four Critical Actions for Schools

## Overview

The Four Critical Actions framework is the Victorian Department of Education's mandated process for responding to incidents, disclosures, or suspicions of child abuse. Every Victorian government school must follow this framework. Catholic and Independent schools use equivalent frameworks.

Source: Department of Education Victoria — Protecting Children — Four Critical Actions

## The Four Critical Actions

### Critical Action 1: Immediate Action

**Purpose**: Ensure safety and preserve evidence.

**What to do**:
1. **Ensure immediate safety** — Separate the child from the alleged perpetrator if they're on site. Do not leave the child alone.
2. **Administer first aid** if needed. Call 000 for medical emergencies.
3. **Preserve evidence** — Do not clean up, move items, or alter the environment where the incident occurred (unless necessary for safety). Note the condition of the environment.
4. **Document initial observations** — Write down what you saw, heard, or were told, using the child's or witness's exact words where possible.

**Decision points**:
- If the child is in immediate danger → Call 000
- If a sexual offence may have occurred → Do not wash the child, change their clothes, or clean up. Preserve evidence for police.
- If the alleged perpetrator is a staff member or volunteer → Contact the Principal immediately. The Principal must contact the DET Employee Conduct Branch.

### Critical Action 2: Report to Authorities

**Purpose**: Notify the appropriate authorities as required by law.

**If immediate danger**: Call 000 (Victoria Police).

**If mandatory reporting applies** (physical or sexual abuse — CYFA s.184):
- Contact DFFH Child Protection (local intake) during business hours
- Child Protection Crisis Line 13 12 78 after hours/24–7

**If Failure to Disclose applies** (sexual offence against child under 16 — Crimes Act s.327):
- Report to Victoria Police (local station or 000)

**School incident reporting**:
- Report via eduSafe Plus or contact ISOC on 1800 126 126
- Extreme severity → Immediately (within 1 hour)
- High severity → Within 3 hours

**Document the report**:
- Date and time of report
- Who you spoke to
- What was reported
- Reference number provided
- Any instructions given

### Critical Action 3: Contact Parents/Carers

**Purpose**: Inform parents/carers of the situation, unless advised otherwise.

**When to contact**: As soon as possible after the incident, **unless** DFFH Child Protection or Victoria Police advise you not to. They may ask you to delay notification to protect an investigation or the child's safety.

**Who contacts**: The Principal or their delegate, in consultation with the Child Safety Officer.

**What to communicate**:
- That an incident has occurred (nature of the incident as appropriate)
- That the school has followed (or is following) the Four Critical Actions
- That a report has been made to [DFFH/Police] (if applicable)
- What support is being provided to the child at school
- What support is available to the family

**Do not**:
- Provide details that could compromise an investigation
- Speculate about what happened
- Share information about other children or staff involved
- Make commitments about outcomes

### Critical Action 4: Ongoing Protection and Support

**Purpose**: Ensure the child's ongoing safety and wellbeing at school.

**Actions**:
1. **Develop a safety plan** — If the alleged perpetrator has access to the school, what measures will prevent further contact?
2. **Provide wellbeing support** — Assign a trusted staff member as a key contact for the child. Offer school counselling or referral to external services.
3. **Hold a Student Support Group (SSG) meeting** — Bring together school staff, parents/carers, and any involved services to coordinate support.
4. **Monitor** — Regular check-ins with the child. Document observations.
5. **Review** — Reassess the safety plan regularly. Is it working? Does anything need to change?
6. **Staff support** — Staff involved may need their own support (EAP, debriefing).

## Documentation Requirements

For every incident following the Four Critical Actions, document:

1. **Chronology of actions** — Timestamped record of what happened and when
2. **Observations and disclosures** — Verbatim where possible
3. **Report details** — Who was reported to, when, reference numbers
4. **Parent/carer contact** — What was communicated, when, by whom, any instructions from authorities
5. **Safety plan** — Documented and shared with relevant staff
6. **SSG meeting notes** — Attendees, decisions, actions

## ISOC Severity Classification

For reporting to the Incident Support and Operations Centre:

| Severity | Examples | Reporting timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Extreme** | Death, life-threatening injury, sexual assault, abduction, weapons incident causing serious injury | Immediately (within 1 hour) |
| **High** | Physical assault requiring medical attention, threat of violence with a weapon, serious self-harm, serious property damage, restraint resulting in injury | Within 3 hours |
| **Medium** | Physical altercation without injury, verbal threat, minor self-harm, minor property damage, bullying requiring escalation | Within 24 hours |
| **Low** | Verbal disagreement, minor rule infraction, isolated non-compliance | Report via routine channels |

## When This Framework Does NOT Apply

The Four Critical Actions specifically address child abuse. Other incidents (general student behaviour, staff conflict, property damage not related to child safety) follow the school's general incident reporting procedures. However, if you're unsure whether an incident triggers this framework, consult your Child Safety Officer — err on the side of following the framework.
