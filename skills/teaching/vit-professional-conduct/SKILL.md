# VIT Professional Conduct

This skill helps VIT-registered teachers understand and apply the Victorian Teaching Profession's Code of Conduct and Code of Ethics, meet their mandatory notification obligations to VIT, and navigate professional conduct situations.

## Before You Begin

Establish what the teacher needs:

1. **What is the situation?** — A specific ethical question, a conduct concern, a notification obligation, or general guidance?
2. **What's at stake?** — Is this a proactive question ("can I do X?") or reactive ("something has happened")?
3. **Registration status** — Provisional or full registration? (The Codes apply equally to both)
4. **School context** — Government, Catholic, or independent? (School-level policies sit under the VIT Codes)

Read `shared-references/vit-policies.md` for the full VIT regulatory framework.

## The Professional Framework

### The Two Codes

VIT-registered teachers are bound by two complementary documents:

**1. The Victorian Teaching Profession's Code of Conduct**
Establishes the enforceable standards of professional conduct. Breaches may result in VIT disciplinary action.

**2. The Victorian Teaching Profession's Code of Ethics**
Articulates the ethical principles and values that underpin the profession.

Both Codes are issued by VIT under the *Education and Training Reform Act 2006*.

### Core Principles

| Principle | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Professional integrity** | Honesty, objectivity, impartiality. Do not misrepresent your qualifications or experience. |
| **Respect** | Treat students, colleagues, parents, and the community with dignity. Maintain a safe, non-discriminatory environment. |
| **Responsibility** | Act in the best interests of students. Maintain professional boundaries. Fulfill your duty of care. |
| **Competence** | Maintain and develop your professional knowledge and practice. Teach within your areas of qualification and competence. |

## Key Conduct Obligations

### Professional Boundaries

Teachers hold a position of trust and authority. Maintain clear professional boundaries:

- **Relationships with students**: Professional, not personal. Do not:
  - Socialise with students outside school activities
  - Connect with students on personal social media
  - Give students gifts beyond those appropriate for a teacher-student relationship
  - Engage in any conduct that could be perceived as grooming
- **Dual relationships**: Where you have a pre-existing relationship with a student (family friend, neighbour, etc.), declare it to your Principal and maintain professional boundaries at school
- **Physical contact**: Only where necessary for student safety, instruction, or wellbeing. Always appropriate, brief, and justifiable.

### Social Media and Online Conduct

Your online conduct reflects on the profession:

- Do not post identifiable information about students, colleagues, or the school without authorisation
- Do not engage with students on personal social media platforms
- Consider whether any public post could be seen by students, parents, or colleagues
- Your private conduct can have professional consequences if it undermines your standing as a teacher

### Conflicts of Interest

- Declare any personal, financial, or family interests that could conflict with your professional duties
- Do not use your position to gain personal advantage
- Private tutoring of your own students should be declared and managed carefully (check school policy)

## Mandatory Notifications to VIT

Teachers must notify VIT of certain matters. These are legal obligations, not optional.

### What You Must Report to VIT (Self-Notification)

You must notify VIT within **30 days** if you:

1. Are **charged with** or **convicted of** any criminal offence (in Australia or overseas)
   - Including traffic offences punishable by imprisonment
   - Excluding minor traffic infringements (parking fines)
2. Have any change to your **Working with Children Clearance** (cancellation, suspension, conditions)
3. Are the subject of **disciplinary action** by another regulatory body (e.g., in another jurisdiction)
4. Have a **medical condition** or impairment that may affect your fitness to teach
5. Have been **dismissed or resigned** following an investigation into your conduct or competence

### School Reporting Obligations

Principals and employers must report to VIT when a teacher:
- Is dismissed or resigns following conduct or competence concerns
- Is the subject of serious misconduct allegations
- Has been stood down or suspended

## If You Receive a Complaint or Are Investigated

### Immediate Steps

1. **Do not panic** — a complaint is not a finding. VIT investigates fairly.
2. **Notify your Principal** — unless the complaint involves them
3. **Contact your union** — AEU (government schools) or IEU (Catholic/independent)
4. **Do not discuss on social media** or with colleagues beyond your support network
5. **Do not contact the complainant** — let the process work
6. **Cooperate with the investigation** — provide requested information truthfully
7. **Seek legal advice** if the matter is serious

### The VIT Investigation Process

1. **Referral**: VIT receives a notification (from you, your employer, a parent, or another source)
2. **Assessment**: VIT determines whether the matter falls within its jurisdiction
3. **Investigation**: VIT may seek information from you, your employer, and other parties
4. **Outcome**: VIT may:
   - Take no further action
   - Impose conditions on your registration
   - Refer you to a formal hearing (for serious matters)
   - Suspend or cancel your registration (for the most serious matters)

### Potential Outcomes

| Outcome | What it means |
|---|---|
| **No further action** | Matter resolved; registration unaffected |
| **Caution** | WARNING recorded; no conditions |
| **Conditions** | Restrictions on your registration (e.g., supervision, no contact with certain students, further training) |
| **Suspension** | Registration temporarily removed; you cannot teach |
| **Cancellation** | Registration permanently removed |

Conditions and cancellation are published on the VIT register (publicly visible).

## Common Scenarios

### Accepting a Student as a Social Media Friend
**Do not do this.** It blurs professional boundaries and may violate the Code of Conduct. Maintain school-approved communication channels only.

### Receiving a Gift from a Student or Parent
Small tokens of appreciation (e.g., a card, a box of chocolates) at end of year are generally acceptable. Significant gifts or repeated gifts should be politely declined or referred to your Principal. Know your school's gift policy.

### A Student Discloses Abuse
Follow the Four Critical Actions framework. Your obligations are:
1. Listen, do NOT promise confidentiality
2. Document verbatim
3. Report to your Child Safety Officer / make a mandatory report
4. Do NOT investigate or question the student

This is covered in detail by the `incident-documentation` skill.

### A Parent Accuses You of Inappropriate Conduct
1. Notify your Principal immediately
2. Document everything — what was said, when, who was present
3. Do not contact the parent directly to discuss the allegation
4. Contact your union
5. Cooperate with any investigation

### You're Charged with a Criminal Offence
Notify VIT within 30 days, even if the matter is unresolved. Failure to notify is itself a breach that can affect your registration.

## Important Principles

### Why professional boundaries are non-negotiable
Teachers are in positions of trust and power. The student-teacher relationship is inherently unequal. What might be acceptable in other workplaces (casual socialising, sharing personal details, connecting on social media) can be boundary violations in teaching. When in doubt, maintain the boundary.

### Why self-reporting matters
VIT's requirement that you self-report criminal charges is strict. A failure to report can result in conditions or cancellation even if the underlying charge is minor. It is always better to report and explain than to not report and have VIT discover it.

### Why the Codes apply both in and out of school
Your registration as a teacher carries obligations that extend beyond school hours. Criminal conduct, public behaviour that undermines the profession, and online conduct can all affect your registration. Teachers are held to a higher standard because of the trust placed in them.

### When in doubt, seek advice
- **Union**: AEU (Australian Education Union) for government; IEU (Independent Education Union) for Catholic/independent
- **Principal or leadership**: For school-level guidance
- **VIT**: For registration questions (1300 888 067)
- **Legal advice**: For serious matters (your union can assist with referral)
