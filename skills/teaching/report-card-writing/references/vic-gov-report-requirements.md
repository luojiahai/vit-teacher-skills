# Victorian Government School Reporting Requirements

## Overview

Victorian government schools must provide written (print or digital) student reports at least twice per year. Reports must be in plain English, include a 5-point age-related scale for core subjects, and follow specific content requirements. Catholic and Independent schools follow similar but not identical requirements.

Source: Department of Education Victoria — Reporting Student Achievement and Progress Foundation to 10 (reviewed 21 January 2025)

## The 5-Point Scale

Mandatory for English, Mathematics, and Science. Optional but common for other learning areas.

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Well above standard | The student consistently demonstrates the knowledge and skills at a level well above the standard expected for their age. |
| Above standard | The student demonstrates the knowledge and skills above the standard expected for their age. |
| At standard | The student demonstrates the knowledge and skills at the standard expected for their age. |
| Below standard | The student is working towards the standard expected for their age. |
| Well below standard | The student is working well below the standard expected for their age. |

**Key rules**:
- This is an **age-related** scale — it measures against the curriculum standard for the student's age, not effort or improvement
- Schools can present the scale as letters, numbers, worded descriptors, or graphics — but if not using worded descriptors, the scale must be explained
- For EAL students: a different 5-point scale must be used (the standard age-related scale is not appropriate)
- For students with disability on adjusted programs: an alternative 5-point scale (learning goals or learning dimensions) may be used with principal endorsement

## English Reporting

English must be reported across three modes:
1. **Reading and Viewing**
2. **Writing**
3. **Speaking and Listening**

Each mode receives its own 5-point scale rating and comment. A student may be At standard in Reading and Viewing but Below in Writing — modes are reported separately.

Minimum reporting: Twice yearly.

## Mathematics Reporting

From Semester 1 2025 (all schools by Term 1 2026): Mathematics Version 2.0 uses a **single aggregated score** against the achievement standard as a whole.

- Previous separate strand reporting (Number & Algebra; Measurement & Geometry; Statistics & Probability) is replaced by one overall progression point
- The first report under Version 2.0 shows achievement only — **no progress line** — because Version 1.0 and 2.0 cannot be directly compared
- Progress indicators return from the second reporting period onwards

Minimum reporting: Twice yearly.

## Science Reporting

- Must be reported at least once yearly from Year 3
- 5-point age-related scale

## Other Learning Areas

- Physical Education: Twice yearly
- Health Education, The Arts, Technologies, Humanities, Capabilities: As taught over the stage/band (at least biennially)
- Languages: At least once yearly

## What Reports Must Show

For each curriculum area reported:
1. **The student's current level of achievement** against the Victorian Curriculum standard
2. **Progress** (where comparable data is available — not applicable in first year of a new curriculum version)
3. **What the student has achieved**
4. **Areas for improvement and suggested strategies for supporting further learning**
5. **Work habits and behaviour** (may be separate from curriculum comments)
6. **Student attendance**

## Plain English Requirement

Reports must be accessible to parents and carers. The Department specifies:
- Use plain, understandable language
- Avoid unexplained educational jargon
- Ensure reports can be understood by parents from diverse backgrounds
- Consider families who speak languages other than English — translation may be needed
- Reports should build parent understanding of the Victorian Curriculum, not assume prior knowledge

## Progress Reporting

- Schools may provide **progress reports** (interim reports) in addition to formal semester reports
- Progress reports are typically shorter — may focus on key areas or students needing additional communication
- Semester reports are the formal, comprehensive record

## Special Circumstances

### EAL Students
- EAL students are assessed against the EAL Companion to the Victorian Curriculum
- An age-related scale is not appropriate — schools use a different 5-point scale reflecting progress in English language learning
- The report should reflect both English language progress AND curriculum achievement as appropriate

### Students with Additional Needs
- For students with significant disability working on a highly individualised program:
  - Schools may use an alternative 5-point scale based on learning goals or learning dimensions
  - This must be agreed with parents/carers and endorsed by the principal
  - The report should reference the student's IEP/ILP goals

### Students Working Above Level
- Students working well above age-expected standards should be reported at their actual achievement level
- For example, a Year 5 student demonstrating the Year 8 Mathematics achievement standard should be reported as "Well above standard" for Year 5

## Student-Teacher-Parent Conferences

- Schools must offer at least one formal opportunity per year for parents to discuss the report with teachers
- This is typically achieved through parent-teacher interviews
- The written report and the conversation are complementary — the report provides the record, the conversation provides the depth
